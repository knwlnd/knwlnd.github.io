Selected Work

ask-it Programme

A four-project global AI programme improving access to IT guidance for a 30-person support organisation and up to 2,800 employees through self-service, internal tooling and automated QA.

Back to projects

Problem

IT teams were handling repeated questions that created clear self-service opportunities, but employees and support staff lacked a fast, reliable way to access accurate internal guidance. The existing Guru knowledge base was poorly maintained, out of date and difficult to navigate.

The ask-it v1 recovery and wider IT Support Quality initiative required both the knowledge source and the AI experience built on top of it to be redesigned. The challenge was to improve human usability while creating content boundaries that reduced hallucinations and mixed guidance in retrieval-augmented generation workflows.

Scope & Role

Global Scope

Built tools and knowledge workflows for a global IT department of approximately 30 people and user-facing services with a potential audience of approximately 2,800 employees.

Project Owner & Builder

Owned the programme and served as the core or sole contributor across product design, development, knowledge architecture, operational recovery, QA and documentation.

Four Project Workstreams

ask-it Internal

Conceived, designed, coded and deployed a Chrome extension giving the global IT Help Desk immediate AI-powered access to internal knowledge and supporting sources.

ask-it v1 Recovery

Inherited a minimally documented Slack bot, reverse-engineered its architecture and built the operating, governance, reporting and knowledge-management model around it.

ask-it v2 Development

Led a ground-up redesign of the user-facing bot, introducing improved workflows, richer interactions, analytics and more maintainable backend integration patterns.

ask-it QA Tool

Designed and built a quality-assurance tool that scored real bot answers and converted failures into structured recommendations for improving the source knowledge.

Project Delivery

Internal Help Desk extension

Built a Chrome extension integrating internal Guru knowledge, monday.com boards, Slack data and vendor documentation. Iterated through more than six prompt versions, displayed response sources as clickable links and added mandatory written feedback for negative ratings.

v1 operational recovery

Reconstructed the inherited Workato and Slack bot architecture, documented it for the first time and established monthly QA, answer review, team assignment, results preparation and leadership reporting processes.

v2 platform design

Developed multi-turn conversation threading, access-request automation, image-assisted troubleshooting, usage analytics and Okta group integration. Refactored n8n workflows to remove hard-coded lists and integrated monday.com item creation and updates through its API.

Automated quality assurance

Built a pipeline to evaluate real questions and answers as correct, partially correct or incorrect, then produce structured Guru improvement recommendations for each failure.

Knowledge System Rebuild

Audit the existing knowledge base

Reviewed 255 existing Guru cards to identify outdated guidance, unclear ownership, duplicated content and articles containing multiple unrelated support scenarios.

Rebuild around single scenarios

Split, rewrote and clarified the original cards into 450 focused articles. Each article was structured around a single support scenario so it could be found quickly, shared directly with users and understood without unrelated context.

Design for reliable retrieval

Reworked content for retrieval-augmented generation, using clearer boundaries and more focused guidance to reduce hallucinations and prevent AI endpoints from combining instructions from unrelated scenarios.

Classify ingestion safety

Created a Bot-Safe Guru Mapping board in monday.com and reviewed all 450 articles to determine whether each was suitable for ask-it ingestion. A curated collection of 196 approved articles was maintained for the conservative v1 production knowledge set.

Programme Approach

Knowledge Architecture

Established a repeatable framework for focused, searchable and AI-ingestible support articles.

Connected Knowledge Graph

Linked remote support, user management, deployment and bot-safe Guru content into a connected system.

Quality Controls

Combined ingestion-safety mapping, feedback capture, monthly reviews and automated QA to manage quality.

System Documentation

Created and maintained system architecture documentation covering knowledge and application components.

Architecture

User-facing experience

Provided a simple entry point for asking operational questions and receiving structured guidance.

Curated knowledge layer

Used focused, classified Guru articles as a controlled source for retrieval and response generation.

Internal review surfaces

Supported QA, commentary and review workflows for improving reliability and operational usefulness.

Analysis tooling

Included supporting tools for endpoint analysis and prompt or response evaluation.

Governance & Measurement

Project Controls

Produced project initiation, risk, communications, rollout, ownership and technical documentation so delivery and handover did not depend on undocumented context.

Operational Reporting

Built results boards and reported monthly on questions, ticket creation, user feedback and automatically resolved tickets to senior IT and departmental leadership.

KPI Framework

Defined targets covering internal resource use, ticket resolution time, systems-team escalations, positive feedback and adoption across the global Help Desk.

Known Failure Modes

Identified and managed noisy data, archived content appearing in answers, multi-topic cross-contamination and the bot responding to messages that were not questions.

Outcomes

255 Cards Fully Rebuilt

Audited, split, rewritten and clarified the complete legacy Guru knowledge set.

450 Focused Articles

Created single-scenario guidance that was easier to find, share, maintain and retrieve accurately.

450 Safety Reviews

Reviewed and classified every rebuilt article for safe use within ask-it ingestion workflows.

500+ QA Evaluations

Executed more than 500 automated response evaluations during April 2026.

Closed Improvement Loop

Connected questions, responses, QA scores, knowledge gaps, content updates and re-testing.

Documented Handover

Left v1 stable and documented, with a defined ownership path, operating model and historical reporting.

The rebuild gave users and IT teams clearer guidance while providing ask-it with a more controlled retrieval source designed to reduce hallucinations and conflicting multi-scenario responses.

Lessons Learned

Reliable internal AI depends on the quality and structure of its source knowledge. Focused content, explicit ingestion controls, system documentation and ongoing review are as important as the user-facing AI experience.
