Selected Work

ask-it Programme

Internal AI enablement work focused on turning recurring operational questions, fragmented guidance and support workflows into practical tools with clear feedback loops.

Back to projects

Problem

Operational teams needed faster access to accurate internal guidance while keeping the experience structured enough to support adoption, quality review and continuous improvement.

The work involved ambiguous requirements, changing stakeholder expectations and the need to balance useful automation with practical controls.

Approach

Workflow Design

Mapped common support patterns, user intents and escalation points into a clearer tool experience.

Feedback Loops

Designed review paths so responses, gaps and edge cases could be captured and improved over time.

Implementation Patterns

Separated user-facing flows, internal review surfaces and supporting analysis tools.

Operational Adoption

Focused on usable interfaces, clear handoffs and outcomes that could fit real team workflows.

Architecture

User-facing experience

Provided a simple entry point for asking operational questions and receiving structured guidance.

Internal review surfaces

Supported QA, commentary and review workflows for improving reliability and operational usefulness.

Analysis tooling

Included supporting tools for endpoint analysis and prompt or response evaluation.

Tool Design Previews

Screenshots are intentionally blurred and degraded. They are included only to show interaction patterns, layout direction and the breadth of supporting tools.

Outcomes

Clearer Access to Guidance

Reduced friction around finding operational answers and understanding the next action.

Improvement Mechanism

Created a path for feedback, QA and refinement rather than treating AI output as a static endpoint.

Reusable Patterns

Established design and implementation patterns that could support adjacent operational workflows.

Practical Adoption

Centred the work on real team usage, operational fit and maintainable handoff points.

Lessons Learned

Useful internal AI tooling depends less on novelty and more on workflow fit, review discipline, clear boundaries and the ability to learn from real usage.
