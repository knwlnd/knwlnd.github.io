Professional Work

Cerby Governance

Application discovery and governance work focused on improving visibility, access control and operational handling across SaaS tools that did not fit neatly into standard identity workflows.

Back to projects

Problem

SaaS growth creates operational risk when ownership, authentication patterns, access lifecycle processes and control coverage are unclear. The challenge was to improve governance without adding unnecessary friction for teams.

Approach

Application Discovery

Identified tools, ownership, access paths and operational gaps across the SaaS environment.

Governance

Defined clearer expectations for ownership, review, control coverage and ongoing operational handling.

SSO & SCIM

Assessed where stronger authentication and lifecycle automation patterns could reduce manual risk.

Operational Controls

Connected governance decisions to practical workflows for onboarding, offboarding and review.

Implementation

Map the application environment

Created a clearer picture of tools, owners, authentication models and lifecycle gaps.

Prioritise operational controls

Focused on practical controls that could be maintained by real teams rather than theoretical coverage.

Connect governance to workflows

Linked control decisions to joiner, mover, leaver and access review processes.

Outcome

The work improved application visibility, lifecycle handling and governance clarity, demonstrating IAM knowledge, process design and practical operational control implementation.

Lessons Learned

SaaS governance works best when it is translated into ownership, review rhythms and lifecycle workflows teams can actually operate.
