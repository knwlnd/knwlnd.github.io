Cerby SaaS Governance

A global programme to bring shadow IT and SaaS applications that could not connect directly to Okta under stronger identity governance through Cerby, SSO, SCIM and application rationalisation.

Back to projects

Problem

The organisation had a large number of shadow IT tools, alongside approved SaaS applications that could not connect directly to Okta for SSO, SCIM provisioning or both. This created gaps in authentication, user lifecycle management, application ownership and visibility.

A core project was established to implement Cerby and close these gaps. I joined the core team to investigate the application estate, determine the appropriate identity pattern for each tool and drive applications through integration, deprecation or redirection.

Scope & Role

Global Scope

Worked across the global SaaS estate, using finance records and procurement data to identify applications that required investigation and governance.

Core Project Contributor

Took ownership of more than 100 shadow IT and non-Okta applications, progressing each through discovery, technical assessment, stakeholder engagement and resolution.

Approach

Application Discovery

Built and worked through the application inventory using finance records, procurement data and monday.com boards to establish usage, ownership and status.

Owner Engagement

Identified application owners and worked with them to understand each tool's implementation, business purpose, user population and access lifecycle.

Technical Assessment

Researched each application's compatibility with Okta and Cerby, covering SSO, SCIM and the practical constraints of integrating unsupported SaaS tools.

Governance Decision

Defined the appropriate outcome for each application: integrate it through the best available identity pattern, deprecate it or redirect users to an approved alternative.

Implementation

Discover and validate

Reconciled financial and procurement records against known applications, found the relevant owners and validated how each tool was being used.

Assess the integration path

Evaluated each tool for direct Okta support and Cerby compatibility, then selected one of four implementation patterns:

  • Okta for both SSO and SCIM.
  • Okta for SSO and Cerby for SCIM.
  • Cerby for SSO and Okta for SCIM.
  • Deprecation or redirection to an approved tool.

Implement and track

Coordinated application owners and technical teams to complete integrations, tracked delivery through monday.com and followed unresolved applications through to a documented outcome.

Technologies

Identity Platforms

Cerby and Okta, using SSO and SCIM patterns suited to each application's capabilities.

Delivery & Application Estate

monday.com boards, finance and procurement records, and a broad global portfolio of SaaS tools.

Outcome

40+ Non-Okta Tools Integrated

Connected more than 40 applications to Cerby, Okta or a combination of both for SSO and/or SCIM.

15 Shadow IT Tools Governed

Brought approximately 15 shadow IT applications under Okta-based SSO and SCIM controls.

45 Tools Rationalised

Deprecated approximately 45 shadow IT tools or redirected their users to approved alternatives.

100+ Applications Assessed

Established ownership, purpose, compatibility and a documented governance outcome for over 100 applications.

The project reduced unmanaged SaaS usage and expanded central identity coverage across applications that could not follow a standard Okta-only integration path.
