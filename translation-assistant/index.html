Independent Project

Translation Assistant

A built MVP for local-first, privacy-preserving speech transcription and translation across multilingual meetings, presentations, videos and live conversations.

Back to projects

Status

The MVP has been built, but has not been deployed or shipped in an enterprise environment. The current work demonstrates the product direction and validates the local-first interaction model before any broader operational rollout.

Concept

The working concept is enterprise-grade multilingual communication without sending meeting audio to the cloud. The product is designed to capture microphone audio, system audio and selected audio sources, then perform transcription, translation, subtitle display and optional summarisation locally on the user's endpoint device.

The goal is not another meeting caption feature. The stronger product direction is a universal local speech understanding layer that can work across Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, Webex, Slack Huddles, local media, presentations and in-person conversations.

Problem

Multinational teams increasingly work across English, Japanese, Spanish, German, French, Portuguese, Mandarin, Korean and other languages. Existing workflows are often fragmented, platform-dependent, cloud-reliant and awkward to use in real operational settings.

Built-in meeting translation tools frequently struggle with business terminology, accents, technical language, multilingual speaker changes, latency and usability. Organisations can end up relying on third-party services, interpreters or expensive meeting tooling despite already paying for the collaboration platforms they use every day.

Product Direction

Local-first Processing

Audio should remain on the endpoint, reducing privacy, compliance, confidentiality and vendor-risk concerns.

Real-time Assistance

Transcribe speech, detect language, translate into a target language and display live subtitles with low latency.

Platform Independence

Function across meeting tools, media sources, presentations and live conversations rather than depending on one platform.

Enterprise Trust

Prioritise privacy, usability, accuracy and reliability for organisations where communication data is sensitive.

MVP Build

Core desktop experience

Built around a macOS-first desktop experience with language selection and a live subtitle workflow.

Local transcription and translation

Focused on local speech recognition and translation rather than cloud-hosted meeting audio processing.

Apple Silicon performance

Used the MVP to explore latency, accuracy, resource use and local-device feasibility on modern Mac hardware.

Validation Questions

Latency

Can transcription and translation feel useful in live meetings without disrupting conversation flow?

Accuracy

How well do local models handle accents, business terminology, technical vocabulary and speaker changes?

Capture Reliability

Can microphone, system audio and selected application sources be captured consistently across real workflows?

Enterprise Fit

Which regulated or privacy-sensitive teams have the strongest need for local multilingual communication infrastructure?

Future Direction

Later product areas could include live summaries, action item extraction, speaker identification, multilingual meeting notes, searchable transcripts, terminology glossaries, acronym recognition, translation memory, managed deployment, policy controls and compliance reporting.

The most promising path is privacy-preserving multilingual communication infrastructure, not a narrow meeting translation add-on.

Founder Fit

The concept aligns with international operations experience, workplace technology delivery, SaaS environments, AI tooling interest and a workflow optimisation mindset. The idea comes from direct exposure to multilingual collaboration problems, procurement tradeoffs and the shortcomings of current platform-native solutions.
